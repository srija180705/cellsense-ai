# Causes of Battery Capacity Fade

## Cycle aging
Repeated charge and discharge cycles gradually reduce usable capacity as the electrode materials degrade. This is the dominant, expected cause of fade in actively used industrial fleets and correlates with total cycle count.

## Elevated temperature
Operating or charging at high temperatures accelerates side reactions that consume active lithium and increase internal resistance. Sustained elevated discharge temperatures are a common driver of faster-than-expected fade.

## Deep discharge and high C-rate
Frequently discharging a battery to very low states of charge, or drawing high currents (high C-rate) under heavy load, imposes additional stress and accelerates capacity loss.

## Diagnostic note
A steep, accelerating decline in State of Health, especially when accompanied by rising discharge temperature, suggests thermal stress compounding normal cycle aging and warrants closer inspection.
