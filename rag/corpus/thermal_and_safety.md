# Thermal Management and Safe Handling

## Discharge temperature limits
Battery discharge temperatures should remain within the manufacturer's specified range. Readings persistently above roughly 38-40 C during normal discharge indicate elevated thermal stress and should prompt inspection of the cooling system and load profile.

## Elevated temperature response
When an asset shows elevated discharge temperature together with declining State of Health, reduce its load, inspect thermal management components, and bring forward its next health assessment.

## Safe handling during replacement
When removing an end-of-life battery, isolate the electrical system, follow lockout procedures, use appropriate personal protective equipment, and store the removed pack in a designated area pending inspection or recycling.

## Critical asset precautions
Assets in the Critical band should not be assigned to demanding duties until replaced, and any sign of swelling, leakage, or abnormal heat must trigger immediate removal from service.
