# Predictive Replacement and Maintenance Policy

## Replacement scheduling by remaining useful life
Schedule battery replacement based on predicted Remaining Useful Life (RUL). When RUL falls below the procurement and installation lead time, initiate replacement immediately to avoid unplanned downtime. Assets with RUL under five cycles should be treated as urgent.

## Monitoring intervals by risk band
Healthy assets follow the standard inspection interval. Watch-band assets should move to a shortened monitoring interval and be added to the replacement plan. Critical assets require close monitoring until replacement is completed.

## Avoiding unplanned downtime
Acting on the highest-risk assets first minimises unplanned downtime and prevents mid-shift failures. Prioritise assets that combine low State of Health with low remaining useful life.

## Thermal checks before high-load assignment
Before assigning any Watch or Critical asset to a high-load or long-duration duty, verify recent discharge temperatures are within normal limits and confirm the cooling path is unobstructed.
